# HR-ANALYTICS-DASHBOARD
Project Overview

-The HR Analytics Dashboard is a Power BI project designed to analyze employee data and identify key factors that influence employee attrition.

-This dashboard helps HR departments monitor workforce trends and make data-driven decisions to improve employee retention and organizational performance.

-The dataset includes employee information such as age, department, salary, job role, education, and years at the company.

Project Objectives

Analyze employee attrition patterns
Identify departments with high attrition
Understand the relationship between salary and employee turnover
Explore attrition trends across age groups and job roles
Provide interactive insights for HR decision making

Dashboard Insights-
Attrition by Education
Shows the distribution of attrition across different educational backgrounds.
Key insight: Employees from Life Sciences and Medical fields show higher attrition.
  
Attrition by Age Group-
Displays attrition trends among various age groups.
Key insight: Highest attrition occurs in the 26–35 age group.

Attrition by Salary Slab-
Analyzes attrition based on employee salary levels.
Key insight: Employees earning Up to 5K salary range show the highest attrition.

Attrition by Years at Company-
Shows how employee experience impacts attrition.
Key insight: Most employees leave within the first few years of joining.

Attrition by Job Role-
Identifies job roles with the highest employee turnover.
Top job roles with high attrition:
Laboratory Technician
Sales Executive
Research Scientist
Sales Representative
Attrition by Gender
Displays the comparison of attrition between male and female employees.
Key insight: Male employees show slightly higher attrition compared to female employees.

Conclusion
-This HR Analytics Dashboard helps organizations identify workforce trends and key factors affecting employee retention.

-By leveraging Power BI visualizations and DAX calculations, HR teams can develop effective strategies to reduce employee attrition and improve workforce stability.
